Swan House: Designed by noted architect Philip Trammel Shutze, and completed in 1928, the Swan House, located in the Buckhead suburb of Atlanta. In 1967 the house opened as a museum and headquarters of the Atlanta Historical Society and features a glimpse into the past with the collection of historical decor and artifacts. Due to discriminatory Jim Crow legislation which was designed to create barriers to education, politics and employment, many household staff members were African American men and women. In 1933, Elizabeth “Lizzie” McDuffie, left the Swan House to work in the President Franklin D Roosevelt While House. There she advocated for racial equality, was a political activist and helped form a worker’s union for government employees.
